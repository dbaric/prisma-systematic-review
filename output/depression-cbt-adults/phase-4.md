### Interpretation of GRADE Certainty Ratings

**HIGH Certainty**: We are very confident that the true effect lies close to the estimate. Further research is very unlikely to change our confidence in the estimate.

**MODERATE Certainty**: We are moderately confident in the estimate. The true effect is likely close to the estimate, but there is a possibility it is substantially different. Further research may change our confidence and may change the estimate.

**LOW Certainty**: Our confidence in the estimate is limited. The true effect may be substantially different from the estimate. Further research is likely to change our confidence and may change the estimate substantially.

**VERY LOW Certainty**: We have very little confidence in the estimate. The true effect is likely substantially different from the estimate. Further research is very likely to change the estimate.

---

### Criteria for Certainty Rating Decisions

**Downgrade Criteria Applied in This Review**:

1. **Risk of Bias Downgrade**: [DATA REQUIRED: ≥50%] of studies with high risk of bias in critical domains → Serious concern (-1 level); [DATA REQUIRED: ≥75%] → Very serious (-2 levels)

2. **Inconsistency Downgrade**: I² = 50–75% with divergent CI directions → Serious (-1); I² > 75% → Very serious (-2). Exception: If subgroup analyses explain heterogeneity (interaction p < 0.05, I² within subgroups < 50%), no downgrade applied.

3. **Indirectness Downgrade**: Majority of studies differ from population/intervention/comparator/outcome specification → Serious (-1); predominantly indirectly addressing the question → Very serious (-2)

4. **Imprecision Downgrade**: 95% CI includes clinically trivial effects (e.g., SMD < 0.3 for depression severity) → Serious (-1); CI very wide crossing both benefit and harm OR <3 studies total → Very serious (-2)

5. **Publication Bias Downgrade**: Egger's test p < 0.10 + funnel plot asymmetry → Serious concern (-1); formal trim-and-fill analysis substantially reduces effect → Very serious

**Upgrade Criteria** (rarely applied in psychotherapy reviews):

- Large effect (SMD > 0.8 or RR > 2.0) without serious limitations → Upgrade (+1)
- Dose-response relationship demonstrated → Upgrade (+1)
- Opposing confounding would bias against the observed effect → Upgrade (+1)
