RESEARCH TOPIC: Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Depression in Adults

RESEARCH QUESTION:
What is the efficacy of cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) compared to standard care, placebo, or other psychotherapies in treating major depressive disorder in adults (18+ years)?

POPULATION:
- Adults aged 18 and older
- Diagnosed with major depressive disorder (MDD) per DSM-5 or ICD-11 criteria
- Any severity level (mild, moderate, severe)
- Any treatment setting (primary care, outpatient mental health, inpatient)

INTERVENTION:
- Cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT)
- Delivered individually or in groups
- Any duration and frequency
- Any delivery method (face-to-face, teletherapy, digital)

COMPARATOR:
- Standard/usual care
- Placebo/attention control
- Other psychotherapies (psychodynamic, interpersonal, supportive)
- Waitlist control
- Treatment as usual

OUTCOMES (PRIMARY):
- Depression symptom severity (measured by validated scales: PHQ-9, BDI-II, HDRS, etc.)
- Remission rates (50% reduction or full remission from baseline)
- Response rates

OUTCOMES (SECONDARY):
- Quality of life
- Functional impairment
- Relapse/recurrence rates
- Treatment dropout rates
- Adverse events

STUDY DESIGN:
- Randomized controlled trials (RCTs)
- Published in English
- Published 2010 or later
- Sample size ≥20 participants

EXCLUSION CRITERIA:
- Studies exclusively in populations with bipolar disorder, psychotic disorders, active substance use
- Studies without comparison group
- Case reports, editorials, commentaries
