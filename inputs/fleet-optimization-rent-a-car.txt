RESEARCH TOPIC: Data-Driven Fleet Management Optimization in Rent-a-Car Operations

RESEARCH QUESTION:
How can machine learning and time series forecasting methods be applied to internal rent-a-car operational data to optimize vehicle distribution, predict maintenance needs, and forecast demand across multiple locations?

POPULATION:
- Rent-a-car companies operating multiple branches (10–15 locations)
- Companies with structured internal reservation and fleet management systems
- Any company size with sufficient historical data for modeling

INTERVENTION:
- Machine learning models for demand forecasting (ARIMA, Prophet, SARIMA, LSTM)
- Predictive maintenance models (survival analysis, Random Forest, XGBoost)
- Vehicle distribution optimization models (regression, classification)

COMPARATOR:
- Baseline heuristics or rule-based fleet management
- Static allocation strategies (fixed number of vehicles per location)
- Reactive (non-predictive) maintenance scheduling

OUTCOMES (PRIMARY):
- Forecast accuracy of vehicle demand per location and category (MAE, RMSE, MAPE)
- Reduction in days where a location lacks a requested vehicle category
- Accuracy of maintenance need prediction (precision, recall, F1)

OUTCOMES (SECONDARY):
- Reduction in unplanned breakdown costs
- Improvement in fleet utilization rate
- Accuracy of demand forecasting across different time horizons (1 week vs. 1 month ahead)
- Vehicle replacement decision accuracy (optimal sell/replace timing)

STUDY DESIGN:
- Empirical study using private internal dataset
- Dataset includes: reservations, fleet records, and service logs
- Three modular approaches evaluated independently and as an integrated system

EXCLUSION CRITERIA:
- Models trained exclusively on publicly available or synthetic datasets
- Approaches without a quantitative baseline for comparison
- Single-location studies not generalizable to multi-branch operations
