# Phase 5: Discussion — PRISMA Items 23a–23d

## Dependencies
> Before starting, read these sections from `output/{theme}/`:
> - `output/{theme}/phase-4.md` → `## GRADE Ratings` and `## Synthesis Results` — to reference your results and GRADE certainty ratings
> - `output/{theme}/phase-3.md` → `## GRADE Setup` — to understand your GRADE framework

## Steps

1. **Item 23a: Interpretation of Findings**
   - Summarize key findings in relation to the research question
   - Consider findings in context of existing evidence
   - Discuss mechanisms if applicable
   - Discuss findings in light of the certainty of evidence (GRADE ratings established in Item 22)
   - For updated reviews: compare key findings with the previous version, noting any changes in direction, magnitude, or precision
   - Address any discrepancies between the registered protocol and what was ultimately done
   → Write to: `output/{theme}/phase-5.md` → `## 23a: Interpretation of Findings`

2. **Item 23b: Limitations of the Evidence Base**
   - Assess quality of evidence for each outcome using GRADE or similar framework
   - Discuss study-level limitations (risk of bias, precision, consistency)
   - Discuss review-level limitations (publication bias, reporting bias, heterogeneity)
   - Assess applicability to different populations/settings
   - Discuss directness/indirectness: whether the populations, interventions, and outcomes studied match the review question
   - Explicitly reference GRADE domains when discussing limitations (risk of bias, imprecision, inconsistency, indirectness, publication bias)
   → Write to: `output/{theme}/phase-5.md` → `## 23b: Limitations of the Evidence Base`

3. **Item 23c: Limitations of the Review Process**
   - Acknowledge limitations in search strategy
   - Note any selective inclusion decisions
   - Discuss impact of data extraction or analysis decisions
   - If narrower scope than planned, justify
   - Acknowledge any deviations from the registered protocol and their potential impact on conclusions
   - Address whether the search was comprehensive enough and what types of evidence might still be missing (e.g., non-English studies excluded, grey literature coverage gaps)
   - Note time-lag bias: studies published after the search date that might change conclusions
   → Write to: `output/{theme}/phase-5.md` → `## 23c: Limitations of the Review Process`

4. **Item 23d: Implications**
   
   - **Implications for Practice:** What do the findings mean for clinical practice, patients, or policy-makers? Reference the certainty level when stating implications. What are current standard treatments? How do results align with/diverge from clinical guidelines? Identify populations for which evidence is stronger/weaker.
   
   - **Implications for Research:** What knowledge gaps were identified? What study designs or populations need further investigation? What methodological improvements are needed? Provide sample size or follow-up duration recommendations.
   
   - For living reviews: state the planned update schedule and trigger conditions for interim updates
   
   → Write to: `output/{theme}/phase-5.md` → `## 23d: Implications`

---

All 4 Discussion sub-items (23a–23d) must be explicitly labeled — do not merge or omit. Use GRADE terminology and reference your certainty ratings throughout. For updated/living review requirements, see `phases/output-format.md` → Special Requirements.
